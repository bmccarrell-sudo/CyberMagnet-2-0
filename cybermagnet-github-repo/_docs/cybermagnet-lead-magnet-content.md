## SECTION 1 — GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE (7 Items)

**[Section header in Electric Blue #007BFF]**
**[Section icon: location pin / map marker]**

Your Google Business Profile is often the first thing a potential customer sees. An incomplete or outdated profile suppresses your rankings and reduces trust before the first conversation.

---

☐ **Your GBP Is Claimed and Verified**
An unclaimed profile means you have no control over how your business appears in Google Search and Maps — and a competitor or stranger could claim it.

☐ **Your Primary Business Category Is Accurate and Specific**
Google uses your category to determine which searches you appear in. "Plumber" is far more effective than "Home Services" — get specific.

☐ **Your Business Description Is Complete and Keyword-Rich**
Your description tells both Google and customers what you do, where you do it, and why they should choose you. Leave it blank and you're leaving rankings on the table.

☐ **You Have At Least 10 High-Quality Photos**
Businesses with 10+ photos get 42% more direction requests than those with fewer. Include exterior, interior, team, and work-in-progress photos.

☐ **Your Services and Products Are Listed with Descriptions**
Google uses your service listings to match you to specific search queries. Every service you offer without a listing is a category of searches you're invisible in.

☐ **You Have a Consistent Flow of Recent Reviews**
A business with 50 reviews from 2021 and nothing recent looks inactive. Fresh reviews — even at lower volume — outperform old ones for both rankings and trust.

☐ **You Respond to Every Review — Including Negative Ones**
Review response signals active management to Google and demonstrates customer care to prospective buyers. Unanswered negative reviews lose you customers every day.

---

**[PRO TIP BOX — Blue border, light blue background]**

💡 **PRO TIP**
The fastest GBP win is usually adding your services with individual descriptions. Most businesses have this blank. It takes 20 minutes and can dramatically broaden the range of searches you appear in.

## SECTION 2 — WEBSITE (7 Items)

**[Section header in Electric Blue #007BFF]**
**[Section icon: browser window / laptop]**

Your website is your 24/7 sales presence. These seven checks identify the issues most commonly responsible for visitors arriving and leaving without making contact.

---

☐ **Your Site Loads in Under 3 Seconds on Mobile**
A one-second delay in load time reduces conversions by 7%. Most local business websites load in 4–8 seconds. Test yours at PageSpeed Insights right now.

☐ **Your Homepage Headline Clearly States What You Do and Who You Serve**
Within 3 seconds of landing, a visitor should know exactly what your business does, where you operate, and who you help. Vague headlines cost you the opportunity before it starts.

☐ **Your Primary CTA Is Visible Without Scrolling**
If a visitor has to scroll to find your phone number or contact button, most won't bother. Your primary call to action belongs above the fold — on every device.

☐ **Your Site Is Mobile-Responsive Across All Screen Sizes**
Over 60% of local search traffic arrives on mobile. A site that requires pinching, zooming, or horizontal scrolling loses those visitors immediately.

☐ **Your Contact Information Is on Every Page**
Phone number, email, and address should appear in your header or footer — accessible from every page without navigation. Hiding contact information loses enquiries.

☐ **You Have Customer Reviews or Testimonials Visible on the Homepage**
93% of consumers read reviews before making a purchase decision. Reviews that exist but aren't displayed on your website are trust signals you're not using.

☐ **Your Site Has a Dedicated Page for Each Core Service**
A single "Services" page covering everything performs far worse in search than individual pages for each service. One page per service = more keyword coverage = more visibility.

---

**[PRO TIP BOX — Orange border, very light orange background]**

🔥 **PRO TIP**
Before spending money on ads or SEO, fix your page speed. A faster website converts more of the traffic you already have — at zero additional cost. Google's PageSpeed Insights is free and tells you exactly what to fix.

## SECTION 3 — SOCIAL MEDIA & DIRECTORIES (7 Items)

**[Section header in Electric Blue #007BFF]**
**[Section icon: network nodes / social grid]**

Inconsistent information across the internet actively hurts your local search rankings. Google cross-references your Name, Address, and Phone number across hundreds of sources — every inconsistency reduces confidence in your listing.

---

☐ **Your Business Name, Address, and Phone Number (NAP) Match Everywhere**
Discrepancies between your website, GBP, Yelp, Facebook, and directories confuse Google's algorithms and suppress your local rankings. Check every listing.

☐ **You Are Listed on the Top 5 Local Directories**
Google Business Profile, Yelp, Bing Places, Apple Maps, and your industry-specific directory (e.g. HomeAdvisor, Zocdoc, Avvo) are the minimum. Missing listings = missing discovery opportunities.

☐ **Your Facebook and Instagram Profiles Are Complete and Active**
Incomplete social profiles signal an inactive business. Your bio, contact information, cover photo, and at least one post in the last 30 days are the baseline.

☐ **You Have Posted at Least Once Per Week in the Last Month**
Consistent social posting keeps your profiles active in the platform algorithm and visible to followers who haven't needed you yet — but might soon.

☐ **You Have a Strategy for Generating New Reviews Consistently**
Reviews don't happen organically at scale. A systematic post-service request — via SMS or email — is how businesses build a review profile that drives trust and rankings.

☐ **Your Social Profiles Link to Your Website**
Every social profile should link back to your website — both as a traffic source and as a signal to Google that your web presence is interconnected and active.

☐ **You Are Monitoring and Responding to Reviews Across All Platforms**
Reviews left unanswered on Yelp, Facebook, and Google are missed trust-building opportunities — and unanswered complaints are the most damaging thing a new visitor can see.

---

**[PRO TIP BOX — Blue border, light blue background]**

💡 **PRO TIP**
Set up a Google Alert for your business name. You'll be notified whenever your business is mentioned online — making it easy to respond to reviews and mentions you'd otherwise miss. Takes 2 minutes. Free.

## SECTION 4 — CONTENT & VISIBILITY (6 Items)

**[Section header in Electric Blue #007BFF]**
**[Section icon: document / content stack]**

Content is how Google understands what your business does in depth — and how potential customers find you before they're ready to call. These six items cover the content signals that matter most for local businesses.

---

☐ **Your Service Pages Include Local Keywords**
Each service page should reference your city or service area naturally — not just your business name. "Emergency Plumber in Dallas" performs far better than "Emergency Plumber."

☐ **You Have at Least One Blog Post or Educational Article Published**
Even one helpful article relevant to your services signals to Google that your site is actively maintained and worth crawling. It also builds trust with visitors researching before buying.

☐ **Your Homepage Meta Title and Description Are Optimised**
The title and description that appear in Google search results are your first impression on every organic visitor. Generic defaults cost you click-throughs every day.

☐ **You Have a Lead Capture Mechanism on Your Website**
A contact form is the minimum. A lead magnet, free quote tool, or audit offer converts visitors who aren't ready to call into leads you can follow up with over time.

☐ **You Have Posted Video Content in the Last 90 Days**
Video content receives 3x more engagement than static images across most platforms and is increasingly favoured by both Google and social algorithms for local businesses.

☐ **You Have a Consistent Content Publishing Schedule**
Inconsistent content publishing (posting 10 times then going silent for months) signals to algorithms and visitors alike that your business isn't active. Even one post per week, consistently, outperforms sporadic bursts.

---

**[PRO TIP BOX — Orange border, very light orange background]**

🔥 **PRO TIP**
Your Google Business Profile posts count as content. If you're not ready to start a blog, start by posting one update per week to your GBP — a photo of a completed job, a seasonal offer, or a customer review. It takes 5 minutes and signals fresh activity to Google.

# PART 4 — DESIGN NOTES (For Canva / Figma / Adobe)

---

## TYPOGRAPHY

**Primary heading font:** Syne Bold / ExtraBold (800 weight) — used for all section headers, CTA buttons, and the document title. Tight letter-spacing (-0.02em to -0.03em).

**Body font:** DM Sans Light / Regular (300–400 weight) — used for all body copy, checklist items, and explanations. Generous line-height (1.65–1.75).

**Accent labels:** DM Sans SemiBold (600 weight), uppercase, 0.12–0.15em letter-spacing — used for section labels, badge text, and "PRO TIP" labels.

---

## COLOUR USAGE

**Navy (#0A1628 / #0A2540):** Page background and card backgrounds. Use the darker navy for the overall document background and the slightly lighter navy for section containers and card fills.

**Electric Blue (#007BFF):** Primary accent. Section headers, checkbox borders, filled checkboxes, progress indicators, link text, and primary graphic elements.

**Orange (#FF7A00):** Secondary accent — used sparingly for urgency, CTA buttons, pro tip box borders, and score band indicators. Never more than 10–15% of any given section's colour footprint.

**White (#FFFFFF) / Off-White (#DCE8F5):** Primary heading and key text colours.

**Gray tones (#8899AA, #445566):** Body copy and secondary text. The darker gray for labels, the lighter gray for body paragraphs.

**Success Green (#00C864):** Score band "Strong" indicator and completion confirmations only.

---

## PDF LAYOUT — PAGE STRUCTURE

**Page size:** A4 (210 × 297mm) or US Letter (8.5 × 11in) — both work.

**Margins:** 18mm (0.7in) on all sides.

**Grid:** Two-column layout for the checklist sections (title column left, items right). Full-width for the cover and scoring table.

**Section headers:** Full-width navy rectangle with white Syne ExtraBold text and a 3px electric blue left border. Consistent height of ~40px.

**Checklist items:** Each item on its own row — checkbox (☐) in electric blue at 14px, bold item title, regular explanation sentence below. 6–8px padding between items.

---

## PRO TIP BOX STYLING

- Background: rgba(0, 123, 255, 0.06) for blue tips / rgba(255, 122, 0, 0.06) for orange tips
- Left border: 3px solid #007BFF or #FF7A00
- Border-radius: 8px
- Padding: 14px 16px
- "PRO TIP" label: DM Sans SemiBold, 10px, all caps, color matching border
- Body text: DM Sans Regular, 12px, gray (#8899AA)
- Icon: 💡 (blue tips) or 🔥 (orange tips)

---

## SCORING TABLE STYLING

- Full-width table, alternating row backgrounds (very subtle: rgba(255,255,255,0.02) on alternating rows)
- Section header rows: navy (#0A2540) background, orange text (#FF7A00), Syne SemiBold
- Checkbox column: right-aligned, 20px wide minimum
- Score bands below table: three separate coloured containers (red/amber/green tones) with matching dot indicator

---

## THUMBNAIL CONSISTENCY GUIDE

All three thumbnails (cover, checklist, scorecard) should share:
- Same background darkness/gradient style (very dark navy base)
- Same level of detail (abstract, not photographic)
- Same absence of text and logos
- Electric blue as the dominant accent in each
- A single orange accent element in each (border stripe, arc segment, or glow point)
- Soft, directional shadows rather than hard edges

This creates a visually coherent product family when all three are displayed together on the landing page or in social media posts.
