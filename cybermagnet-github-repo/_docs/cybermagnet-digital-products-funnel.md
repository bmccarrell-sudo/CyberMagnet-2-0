## 1. PAGE PURPOSE & CUSTOMER JOURNEY POSITION

The Digital Products Catalog sits at the **awareness-to-consideration** transition in the CyberMagnet funnel. It serves three distinct audience segments simultaneously:

- **Cold traffic** (from ads, blog, or SEO): Low-commitment entry point — a $37–$97 product is far easier to say yes to than a $300+ system, making this the fastest path from stranger to paying customer.
- **Warm traffic** (blog readers, email subscribers, social followers): The catalog converts content consumers into buyers by offering the tools behind the content they've been reading.
- **Systems comparison shoppers**: Visitors who aren't ready to invest in a full done-for-you system can start with a relevant product, experience the quality of CyberMagnet's work, and upgrade later.

**What it accomplishes:**
1. Generates immediate revenue from lower-commitment buyers
2. Creates a customer relationship that can be upgraded to Systems or DFY services
3. Demonstrates expertise through the product quality itself
4. Builds an email list of buyers (the highest-quality segment)

## 4. DESIGN DIRECTION

**Colour palette:** Maintain the full CyberMagnet design system. For the digital products section specifically, gold (#c9a84c) is the primary accent — differentiating it from the blue-accented Systems pages and the orange-accented funnel pages. This signals "premium catalog" vs "action/urgency."

**Product card thumbnails:** Each product uses a unique dark gradient background (distinct hue per category), a grid overlay, a gradient-to-transparent overlay from bottom, and a large emoji icon. This creates visual variety within a consistent format without requiring custom imagery.

**Typography:** Syne 800 for all headings, DM Sans 300/400/500 for body. Maintain exact sizing from global.css.

**Spacing:** Section padding uses the standard `clamp(64px, 8vw, 104px)` vertical rhythm. Product cards use 20–22px internal padding. Grid gaps of 22px keep cards distinct without excessive white space.

**Visual hierarchy priority:**
1. Featured product (spans 2 columns, taller thumbnail, larger title)
2. Individual product cards (consistent 3-column grid)
3. Bundle card (full-width, gradient border animation, price block offset)
4. Trust signals (4-cell proof strip, 3 testimonials)
